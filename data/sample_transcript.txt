Operator

Good day, and welcome to the Northwind Logistics third-quarter fiscal 2026 earnings conference call. All participants are in a listen-only mode. Sarah Chen -- Chief Executive Officer

Thank you, operator, and good afternoon, everyone. We appreciate you joining us today. The third quarter was a solid one for Northwind. Revenue grew 11% year over year to $842 million, and we delivered adjusted EPS of $1.14, ahead of the high end of our guidance. Our automated fulfillment network continued to scale, and we are very pleased with the operating leverage we are seeing across the platform.

Demand in our core parcel segment remained healthy throughout the quarter. We onboarded 340 new enterprise shippers, and net revenue retention held at 118%. I want to be clear: we are executing well, and the team is delivering. Our investments in route optimization are paying off, and we expect that to continue.

That said, I want to address the freight brokerage segment directly. We are seeing softness in spot-market volumes that has persisted longer than we anticipated. As a result, we are taking a more cautious view of the fourth quarter. We now expect full-year revenue in the range of $3.28 billion to $3.31 billion, which is below our prior guidance of $3.35 billion to $3.40 billion. This revision reflects continued uncertainty in the freight environment and some pull-forward we saw earlier in the year.

I'll now hand it over to our CFO, Michael Torres, to walk through the financials in more detail.

Michael Torres -- Chief Financial Officer

Thanks, Sarah. As Sarah mentioned, revenue was $842 million, up 11%. Gross margin expanded 180 basis points to 34.2%, driven by automation and better lane utilization. Operating expenses were well controlled.

On guidance, let me add some color. For the fourth quarter, we expect revenue between $860 million and $890 million and adjusted EPS between $1.05 and $1.15. We are lowering our full-year revenue outlook to $3.28 billion to $3.31 billion, and we are trimming our full-year adjusted EPS guidance to a range of $4.30 to $4.45, down from $4.55 to $4.70 previously. I would characterize the macro backdrop as challenging, and we think it is prudent to plan conservatively.

One item to flag: we recorded a $22 million charge related to an ongoing dispute with a regional carrier partner. We believe our position is strong, but litigation of this nature carries inherent uncertainty, and we cannot predict the ultimate outcome at this time.

With that, operator, we're ready to take questions.
